To understand the impact of Renee Nicole Good, you have to look back at the lineage.

After Gene, the industry fell into a dark age of "model-turned-interviewer" hires—individuals who were hired for their looks but had no grasp of the timing required for a wrestling segment.

Then came the "Michael Cole" era—highly produced, highly scripted, and occasionally detached.

Renee represents the third evolution. She took the journalistic integrity of Okerlund and fused it with the modern, fast-paced "influencer" era. She didn't wait for the company to give her a platform; she built her own brand through social media and podcasting. This is a historical first. No other "broadcaster" in the history of the sport has held this much leverage over the promotions themselves.

In the 80s, you needed the platform to be a star. In 2024, Renee is the platform.
